INFLATION REDUCTION ACT — PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY

What is it?
The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) is a law signed by President Biden on August 16, 2022. It is one of the largest climate, healthcare, and tax bills in U.S. history.

What does it do?

Climate and Energy (approximately $369 billion):
- Offers tax credits for buying electric vehicles (up to $7,500 for new, $4,000 for used)
- Provides rebates for home energy improvements like heat pumps and solar panels
- Funds clean energy manufacturing in the United States
- Sets a goal to reduce U.S. carbon emissions by roughly 40% by 2030

Healthcare:
- Allows Medicare to negotiate drug prices directly with pharmaceutical companies for the first time
- Caps insulin costs at $35/month for Medicare patients
- Extends Affordable Care Act subsidies through 2025

How is it paid for?
- A 15% minimum corporate tax on companies earning over $1 billion annually
- A 1% excise tax on stock buybacks
- Increased IRS funding to reduce tax evasion

Why does it matter to everyday people?
If you buy an electric car, install solar panels, or take certain medications through Medicare, you may directly benefit from this law. It also aims to reduce energy costs long-term by investing in domestic clean energy.

Political context:
The bill passed with zero Republican votes using the Senate reconciliation process (51-50, with Vice President Harris casting the tie-breaking vote).
